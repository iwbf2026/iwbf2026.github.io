Destination South of France

Côte d’Azur

The Côte d’Azur is known for its Mediterranean climate, offering warm, sun‑filled summers and mild winters that draw tourists from around the globe. The region is synonymous with luxury, boasting high‑end resorts, gourmet dining experiences, and exclusive shops selling designer merchandise. Glamour permeates the air, especially during the Cannes Film Festival, when movie stars and celebrities descend upon the coastline, adding a touch of Hollywood sparkle.

Yachts are a common sight in the turquoise waters, a testament to the affluent lifestyle that has become a hallmark of the Côte d’Azur. The region's azure skies, charming Provençal villages, and lavender fields paint a picture of quintessential French beauty. There's also an intriguing blend of tradition and modernity in the area, with historic architecture sitting side by side with contemporary art galleries and museums. With its vibrant nightlife and rich cultural offerings, the Côte d’Azur is truly an embodiment of French elegance and joie de vivre.

Antibes

Nestled between Nice and Cannes, Antibes is a historic seaside town famed for its 16th‑century ramparts and the Picasso Museum housed in the former Château Grimaldi. Stroll through the winding lanes of the Old Town to discover lively Provençal markets, craft boutiques, and pastel‑coloured facades. The Port Vauban marina—Europe’s largest pleasure boat harbor—teems with luxury yachts against a backdrop of sandy beaches and the sparkling Mediterranean. Antibes combines centuries‑old charm with vibrant cafés, seaside promenades, and a calendar of cultural events, making it an essential stop on the Côte d’Azur.

EURECOM

EURECOM is a graduate school and research center in digital sciences located in Sophia Antipolis, a major European technology park on the French Riviera. It is a consortium that brings together several academic and industrial partners. Established in 1991, EURECOM has developed a reputation for cutting‑edge research and high‑quality graduate education.

We are sincerely happy and looking forward to hosting the workshop at EURECOM. It will be our pleasure to welcome all participants to the Marconi Amphitheatre, conveniently located at the entrance of our facility. The entrance hall will serve as a lively venue for the poster session, where researchers and scholars can share their work, stimulating in‑depth discussions and sparking new ideas. Participants will also enjoy coffee breaks and lunches on our beautiful terrace, providing opportunities to relax and network between sessions.

By bus The Sophia Express – line 630 - is a direct bus which runs from the airport (Terminal 2) to Sophia Antipolis (bus stop 'Les Templiers'); 10 minutes walking distance from EURECOM. Alternative choices include hiring a VTC (voiture avec chauffeur) for roughly 45 euros, using Uber which can cost between 40 and 50 euros, and taking a taxi that could charge up to 60 euros.
